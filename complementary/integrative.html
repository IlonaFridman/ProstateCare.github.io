What is Complementary and Integrative Treatment?

Exploring complementary and integrative therapies can be a helpful part of your care. Your care team can support you and answer questions.

To help you navigate this space, we summarized therapies based on evidence-based sources in the infographic below.

Important: Always check with your doctor before starting any new therapy or supplement, as some may reduce the effectiveness of your treatment or cause harmful side effects.

Recommended Complementary and Integrative Treatment

Fatigue

Energy & Stress
Try
  • Yoga
  • Tai Chi
  • Massage Therapy
Why it helps
Increases energy and reduces stress.
Interactions
No known

Pain

Comfort
Try
  • Acupuncture
  • Acupressure
  • Massage
  • Meditation
  • Physical Therapy
Why it helps
Reduces pain perception; promotes relaxation.
Interactions
No known

Anxiety & Stress

Mind–Body
Try
  • Acupressure
  • Meditation
  • Deep Breathing
  • Music Therapy
Why it helps
Lowers stress; boosts mood.
Interactions
No known

Insomnia (Trouble Sleeping)

Sleep
Try
  • Guided Imagery
  • Herbal Teas (e.g., chamomile)
Why it helps
Promotes relaxation and better sleep.
Interactions
Some herbal products, including certain teas, can interact with medicines (for example, hormone therapy). Check with your doctor.

Neuropathy (Nerve Pain)

Nerve
Try
  • Acupuncture
  • Physical Therapy
  • Essential Oils
Why it helps
Relieves pain and improves nerve function.
Interactions
Essential oils can be toxic if ingested.

Digestive Issues

Gut
Try
  • Probiotics
  • Peppermint Oil
  • Dietary Adjustments
Why it helps
Eases bloating, diarrhea, and constipation.
Interactions
Probiotics may interact with antibiotics.
